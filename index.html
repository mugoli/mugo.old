Mugoli Samba​ is a fully bilingual communications professional who bridges the gap between url and irl. She understands that messaging and presentation are key to connecting with real people, and creating tangible results.


Her strategic communications experience touches social media content creation and management, blogging, web management, event organizing, and media relations, among other tasks. She spent two years working on the National Capital Commission's strategic communications team, and has used these skills in a variety of settings, notably to help create a communications strategy for Uniterra Haiti. More recently, Mugoli created and implemented a social media strategy for the Media and Mass Atrocity: The Rwanda Genocide and Beyond conference, where she got the #RwandaAndBeyond hashtag trending across Canada.

Her journalism experience has taken her from coastal Haiti to spending ten days sailing across the St. Lawrence River on the Picton Castle for Radio-Canada Québec. Some of her bylines include the Windsor Star, The 405, and The Charlatan. Some of her earlier interests include the Ottawa arts community - while in high school, she founded a blog about the city’s urban arts scene, and organized shows featuring local artists. Her first official byline came from Grammy-nominated producer boi-1da's blog, boi-1da.net, at the age of 16.


She will be graduating with a combined honours degree in Journalism and African Studies from Carleton University in 2018. Her African Studies research interests focus on the African Great Lakes region - more specifically the Democratic Republic of the Congo, from which her parents are from.
